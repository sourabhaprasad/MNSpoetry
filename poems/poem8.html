Sand and Sea

The sea and the sand The depth is feared The eye opaque to the boundless strech The waves ebbs n flows within you Gushing to be held by the sand And then resolving all of you in it. Are you engulfing the anchored sand or The unquenched sand taking in all of you? For one of them falls into the porous arms of the other. One fills the voids, The other weighing the depths A monotonous wave hitting the once rock Inseparable yet solitary.
